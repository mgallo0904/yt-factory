# ChatGPT vs Claude vs DeepSeek vs Kimi

## Hook
I ran the same ten prompts through every major AI model. Same questions, same tasks, same expectations. What came back was not what I expected. Claude destroyed ChatGPT on long documents. Kimi outperformed everybody on coding. DeepSeek was faster than all of them combined. And ChatGPT, the one most people are paying for, was actually mediocre in half the categories. Here is the brutal truth about which model you should actually be using for each task, and which one you are probably wasting money on.

## Content

### The Coding Test
I gave every model the same complex coding task: build a web scraper that handles pagination, rate limiting, and data cleaning, all in Python. Claude nailed the architecture but got the pagination logic wrong. ChatGPT wrote clean code but missed edge cases. DeepSeek flew through the task in seconds but the error handling was lazy. Kimi was the surprise winner. It not only wrote working code, it anticipated the rate limiting problem before I even mentioned it and included a retry decorator and exponential backoff automatically. If you write code daily, Kimi is not just an option. It might be the only model that actually thinks like a developer.

### The Long Document Analysis
I dropped a forty-page legal contract into each model and asked for a summary of liabilities and unusual clauses. ChatGPT hit its context window halfway through and started hallucinating clauses that did not exist. DeepSeek scanned the whole document but summarized at a high school reading level, missing nuance. Kimi got through the whole thing but the summary was shallow. Claude was in another league entirely. It identified three liability clauses I missed, flagged a non-standard indemnification paragraph, and suggested follow-up questions for the lawyer. If your work involves reading long documents, contracts, research papers, or anything over ten pages, Claude is not optional. It is the only model built for this exact job.

### The Speed Benchmark
I ran a series of reasoning questions, creative writing prompts, and data analysis tasks. The winner for raw speed was DeepSeek. Every single response came back in under two seconds. The trade-off was occasional glibness. It would skip steps it deemed obvious, which is fine until you actually need those steps. ChatGPT was middle of the pack on speed and quality. Claude was the slowest but the most thorough. Kimi landed in the sweet spot: fast enough to not break flow, thorough enough to trust without double-checking. If you are burning through hundreds of prompts a day, speed matters. But if you are doing deep work, the extra thirty seconds for Claude is worth it.

### The Creative Writing Gap
I asked every model to write a product launch email in a specific tone: confident but not arrogant, technical but accessible. ChatGPT defaulted to generic marketing speak. Claude swung too formal. DeepSeek kept inserting emoji and exclamation points like a social media intern. Kimi was the closest to usable, but it still needed editing. None of them are replacing human copywriters yet. The gap here is real and it is not closing as fast as the hype suggests. If your job is creative writing, treat these models as first-draft generators, not final products. The editing step is still where human judgment reigns.

### The Honest Verdict
Here is my actual recommendation. Do not pay for just one model. Use Kimi for coding and general reasoning. Use Claude for long documents and complex analysis. Use DeepSeek when you need speed above all else. And use ChatGPT only if you are already locked into its ecosystem. If you are paying twenty dollars a month for ChatGPT Plus and never touching the other models, you are leaving capability on the table. The best AI strategy is multi-model, not loyal. Test them all. Keep the ones that earn their keep. And never let a subscription autopilot your decision-making.
